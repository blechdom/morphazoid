Inspiration disclosure

THROATAZOID is very, very inspired by Pink Trombone—its playful, interactive approach to shaping a synthetic vocal tract is foundational to this experiment.

Choose microphone, internal glottis, or hybrid excitation and awaken the organism. Microphone and hybrid modes may request microphone access. One root airway feeds a shared manifold, which branches into multiple complete mouths. Drag a mouth diamond left or right to change its branch-airway length and toward or away from its center line to change aperture. Cross-coupling leaks breath and resonance between adjacent mouth airways. Drag tongue diamonds and nose gates to shape articulation. Use the Anatomy and Articulation controls for keyboard-accessible adjustment. On the canvas, Left and Right select a mouth, Up and Down change aperture, brackets change branch length, and Enter seals or opens the selected mouth. Enable Type to speak to hold A, E, I, O, U, K, T, P, S, F, M, or N, with Q for glottal stop, X for SH, and G for NG, as momentary articulations. The direct stage controls adjust articulation place, aperture, and voicing while the pressure meter reports stored tract pressure. Press Escape to sever audio.
