# Morphazoid

Nine geometric instruments for sounding contact, recursion, and recorded motion.

**Shape** is a two-dimensional instrument: one to twelve point playheads trace the boundary, one to four independently orientable scan lines read contour intersections, or center-rooted radar rays rotate through every contour hit. Radar position is expressed as an angle from 0–360°. Every playhead type can loop or ping-pong, and shape rotation has its own direction and loop/ping-pong transport. Point and radar spacing markers carry independent forward/reverse arrows, while each line marker carries its own 90-degree orientation toggle. A Sides / points count of 1 is a vertex-free circle, 2 is an open line, and 3–32 can be polygons or stars.

**Lattice** turns a curved isohedral tiling into one deduplicated edge field. A single centered line is the only playhead: the infinite pattern moves beneath it and sounds every edge it intersects simultaneously. Its independent motion bearing can snap from right-to-left to up-to-down or rotate continuously between them without changing the reader line angle. It never walks or serializes the lattice. All 72 Tactile isohedral families are available, with their native shape parameters and independently bendable J, U, and S edge classes; symmetry-locked I edges remain straight.

**Spiral** maps an editable isohedral tessellation into log-polar space. Radius, angle, or multi-turn spiral readers intersect the warped edge field, and each contact can map radius, reader position, orientation, or incidence into continuous, percussive, Shepard, FM, or PM voices.

**Solid** extends the intersection instrument into 3D. Eight wireframes—including cube, octahedron, prism, sphere, and torus—can be stretched and skewed before an oriented 2D plane cuts them. Segment/plane contacts become voices, while X/Y/Z rotation and surface yaw/pitch each have independent play and speed controls.

**Hyper** uses the same idea in 4D. Tesseract, hypersphere, hyperpyramid, and Klein-bottle wireframes can be stretched through X/Y/Z/W. XW, YW, and ZW rotation run independently while a moving W hyperplane sounds the intersected 4D segments.

**L-System** traces recursive turtle grammars including Pythagorean trees, Koch and Sierpiński forms, Hilbert and Gosper curves, Cantor rests, Lévy C, dragon, and terdragon curves. Its default sine begins as one voice and bifurcates through 2, 4, 8, 16, and onward; branch power is rescaled so the combined level stays bounded, while cumulative branch angle determines inherited pitch. Rendering starts from a proven 128-voice budget, then admits more voices in guarded steps when the active structure asks for them and measured audio load has headroom, up to a 4,096-voice hard guard. The Structure pane can read the final expansion, give every iteration equal sequential time, phase-lock them together, accumulate them, or stagger them as a canon. Turn asymmetry creates unequal pitch branches, while branch length taper controls only grammar levels marked by `>` and `<`.

**Julia** finds every closed boundary of an escape-time Julia set with marching squares and traverses the longest loop at constant spatial speed. Each signed left or right turn bends a seventeen-partial Shepard tone in the corresponding direction; at the default mapping, one complete signed revolution crosses four cyclic octaves. The listening default is `c = -0.788 + 0.1191i` at 32 escape iterations, a 320² boundary grid, raw contour treatment, and 0.017 circuits/second. A quieter companion keeps the turn melody intact while mapping vertical location from a minor-third to a perfect-fifth interval, so the set's exact antipodal halves remain audibly related without becoming identical at the four-octave wrap. Complex constant, grid resolution, signed smoothing/reduction, turn polarity, pitch sensitivity, and corner glide remain playable controls. Wheel/slider zoom resamples the complex-plane viewport at full grid resolution, dragging empty stage space (or Shift-dragging) pans it, and Reset view returns to the complete set without breaking the sounding loop. Its Self-similarity lab takes a short playhead-centered boundary motif through the exact inverse branches ±√(z−c), which predict where related arcs occur. It overlays all 2<sup>depth</sup> locations, highlights and auditions a selected repeated-sign chain, measures local tangent-pitch fidelity after normalized-time resampling, reports the nearest low-period repelling target as a separate diagnostic, and gives multiscale chorus, delayed curvature canon, timbre-separated wavelet bands, sequential whole-arc orbit, and aligned similarity-harmony distinct mono-safe listening grammars. The reproducible report adds 40-way distractor retrieval and independent zoom-field checks; local percentages in the page are signal proxies, not claims of human recognition.

Julia presets include the Unit circle, Cauliflower, San Marco, Basilica, the exact period-three Douady rabbit/dragon, Co-rabbit and Airplane centers, and the golden-mean Siegel disk. Airplane automatically raises the minimum grid and reduces contour simplification so its small Fatou components remain playable. Zero-interior examples such as the `c=i` dendrite and `c=-2` Chebyshev interval remain intentionally absent until the renderer supports potential or distance contours.

**Lumber** is a live audio looper drawn as thin, differently colored concentric rings. Each ring has inline direction, relative volume, centered L/R pan, mute, solo, and delete controls. Straight radial handles apply duration-preserving local pitch: inward lowers and outward raises only adjacent loop sections. Additional controls provide movable playback heads, per-ring clocks, one-click global length/phase sync, per-ring filtering, and optional 3D depth. A pale outer Delay ring can be toggled for the complete mix: its neutral shape is dry, inward pulls shorten the echo, and outward pulls add feedback and wet level. It has an independent rotation transport and stereo spread control.

**Fractaphone** is a live recursive microphone. Each delayed descendant is filtered, softly saturated, and split back into a bounded two-branch feedback matrix, so the voice progressively changes without allowing the loop gain to exceed its safety ceiling. Tunnel, Bloom, Choir, and Fray presets reshape time, depth, branching, mutation, and stereo spread. The primary input button starts the microphone once, then pauses or resumes new sound while the existing recursive tail continues; Stop audio is the separate full disconnect. The canvas draws the audible generations as a branching family of waveform capsules, and Capture records the processed stereo result locally in the browser.

Lattice keeps its prototile editor permanently visible. Movable corners are projected onto the selected family's native Tactile parameter space, while an overlap guard rejects self-crossing edits and a density guard limits visual/audio overload.

Head spacing can be edited while the instrument runs: drag the markers in the compact head-layout control, or reset them to an equidistant distribution. The Sides / points control chooses circle, open line, polygon, or star topology; signed controls then stretch, skew, round outward, or bow inward, with a center reset beside each slider. Reset form restores those shaping parameters without changing the side/point count. Every transform is normalized so the whole rotating form remains inside the stage. Drag the contour itself to spin it whenever auto-rotation is off.

Sound modes are exclusive, and every active contact has its own synth path. Except for the circle, continuous synths use an editable per-contact Corner Amplitude ADSR across each directed corner-to-corner interval, with short Pluck, Note, Sustain, and Pad presets and an on/off control that defaults to on. The editor reports each node's current millisecond endpoint from the trigger; Point playback uses the form's actual edge timing, while nonlinear Line and Radar motion uses a clearly marked even-corner nominal estimate. Swell values instead show symmetric offsets from the corner peak. Zero playhead speed is shown as stopped rather than infinite time; if Line or Radar contacts are moving from form rotation alone, the editor reports that no stable millisecond estimate exists. Release at zero rests the synth until the next corner trigger; a nonzero release holds that level until the next trigger. Optional corner swell mirrors the envelope from each adjacent edge midpoint into the corner peak and back out again, using each edge's own length. The evaluated envelope level can then be scaled per Synth by a selected stage-position or contour source without changing A/D/S/R timing. The circle is a continuous, vertex-free contour with constant gain, no vertex handles, and no ADSR. Percussion instead produces discrete corner strikes through a separate absolute-time Percussion ADSR with draggable millisecond nodes and the same four presets. Strike level scales each mapped trigger before Master level, and Attack noise blends in a short bounded noise transient. Shepard Glissandi use seventeen overlapping octave partials around a fixed Base-frequency anchor, so the transport supplies the cyclic pitch motion without a competing geometry-pitch curve; unwrapped travel keeps fractional octaves-per-circuit continuous across the contour seam. FM and PM use their full index/depth controls by default, with optional geometry-driven scaling and independent source choices. Active synths are level-normalized, and the Master level beside the top Audio button adjusts the combined output.

Lattice reuses the same click-safe voice pool, synth modes, pitch curves, stereo mapping, level normalization, and lazy Web Audio lifecycle. Every selected contact is one patch, with guarded onset accents and crossfaded percussion retriggers. Dense fields retain a centered adjacent contact window so tighter visual spacing produces tighter pitch spacing, while density also increases the exact lattice-cycle rate. Form edits temporarily suppress onset detection to avoid ripping or level surges.

Shape mapping always uses fixed stage/screen axes; there is no form-local coordinate mode. Pitch defaults to vertical position with higher points producing higher pitches, and can instead follow horizontal position or distance from center through a draggable five-node response curve with linear, exponential, logarithmic, smooth, and inverted presets. Stereo position can use the same three dimensions, with direction flip and width controls. Corner ADSR level, FM index, PM phase depth, and percussion amplitude can independently use Direct, Left → right, Up → down, Center → edge, corner sharpness, crossing angle, or contour position sources; percussion also retains inner/outer polarity. The Output dashboard exposes the live values and planned Web MIDI, OSC, and JSON-stream destinations. Every page starts from its baseline settings when loaded, and the bottom-of-panel reset returns the complete instrument to that baseline.

On small screens the stage stays visible while the controls scroll independently. Play, Form, Sound, Mapping, and Output are collapsible so the most useful controls can stay close together during performance.

Morphazoid is built with plain HTML, CSS, JavaScript modules, Canvas, and Web Audio—there is no framework or build step.

Continuous voices render in an AudioWorklet. Moving geometry sends short look-ahead trajectories to that render thread, while transport time prefers the audio clock and the canvas follows it. Canvas resolution is pixel-budgeted on large/retina windows so display work cannot grow without bound.
